Accountability at Terex

What it means and why it matters

Accountability at Terex means delivering on commitments, taking ownership of outcomes, and holding yourself and others to results — not just intentions. For people leaders, it means setting expectations clearly enough that teams know when they're on track, and coaching with consistency so follow-through becomes the norm.

Activities support our culture of holding teams to standards of accountability from a place of care, not punishment.

Milestones

The three milestones

Milestone 1
Setting Clear Standards

Leaders build the habit of coaching their direct reports to recognize and reinforce specific standards with their own teams, rather than assuming accountability is understood.

Milestone 2
Sharing Status Updates

Leaders build the habit of proactively keeping their direct reports informed on decisions, changes, and open items — before assumptions fill the gap and drive the wrong behavior.

Milestone 3
Following Through on Commitments

Leaders build the habit of visibly modeling the specific behaviors they are asking their teams to adopt, so accountability is demonstrated, not just directed.

Sample Activities

What participants actually do

Each activity is applied in a real leadership interaction before it's marked complete.

Call Out the Standard Behind the Number
Drive accountability by naming a standard behind a good result so direct reports can carry it forward to their own teams.
What you'll do
In your next operations review, production check-in, or one-on-one, name a specific standard one direct report's team is following, then say exactly why that makes their team accountable.
Desired outcome
Your direct report leaves the conversation knowing what standard drove the result and has a clear plan to reinforce it with their own team.
Separate the Data from the Diagnosis
Drive accountability by coaching your direct report to look at data before assuming their team is responsible for a recurring gap.
What you'll do
In an operations review, production check-in, or one-on-one, name what the data shows before naming any cause, then ask a question that makes your direct report separate what's confirmed from what they're assuming about their team.
Desired outcome
Your direct report owns the next step of verifying the actual cause before the team is held accountable for it.
Escalate a Barrier
Drive accountability by escalating barriers to the right team.
What you'll do
In a staff meeting, cross-functional leadership meeting, or one-on-one with the person who owns the resolution, name the specific barrier and press for a named owner and a resolution date before the conversation ends.
Desired outcome
The barrier leaves the conversation with a named owner and a committed date, so progress can be tracked and the team isn't blocked waiting on an unresolved dependency.
