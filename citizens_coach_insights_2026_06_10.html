01 — Coach Focus Areas

What the coach is consistently working on

Across 71 sessions in the last two weeks, four coaching moves appear repeatedly — the habits the coach is building session by session.

01

Pre-visit 360 review discipline

Nearly every activity starts with the coach sending participants into 360 before the visit to identify a specific candidate. When participants skip the prep or arrive saying "I'm done," the coach checks whether the review actually happened before moving forward.

"Before you head to Crestwood, I want you to open 360 for that location and sort by open date. Look for anything sitting at 30 days or more."
02

Redirecting scope and candidate selection

Participants frequently offer candidates that don't qualify — installation projects, HR cases, WOs that are on track. The coach evaluates each one and redirects to a maintenance or repair WO that is genuinely stalled, without letting the participant take an easy exit.

"That one's only about 3–4 weeks old and has a clear explanation — that's actually on track, not stalled. Take one more look."
03

Holding the expectation when 360 comes back empty

When 360 shows nothing at the target site, the coach doesn't let participants forfeit. It pivots to a second location, or redirects attention to on-site observation — keeping the activity alive and the diagnostic pressure on.

"Since Princeton is clean, let's shift to a different location. Which of your 45 sites do you know has something that's been nagging?"
04

Root cause and 360 documentation specificity

The recurring coaching move is "why has this been open so long?" — not just "did you log it." The coach pushes participants past general notes to named owners, target dates, and a categorized root cause before accepting the activity as complete.

"A named owner and a target date. Something like: 'ABC Paving — address drainage and regrade affected areas by [date].' That way there's accountability and something to follow up against."

03 — What the Coach Is Teaching Us

Patterns emerging from the coaching data

The coaching transcripts reveal consistent behavioral gaps the coach is surfacing — and what it is doing to close them.

🔍

Familiarity suppresses observation

FMs who visit a site regularly stop seeing what's always been there. The coach actively reframes this: the longer you've known a site, the more you need to look deliberately. Senior leaders face this trap most acutely.

📋

Prep separates reactive from proactive

The 360 review before a visit is the habit the coach builds most consistently. FMs who arrive knowing their candidate make faster, sharper decisions on-site. Those who skip prep spend the visit hunting.

🎯

Root cause vs. symptom logging

Participants default to describing what they see, not why it persists. The coach repeatedly pushes past the surface observation to ask what category of failure is at work — vendor gap, deferred maintenance, never formally flagged.

📝

Documentation specificity drives accountability

Vague 360 notes ("I'll monitor it," "contractor to address") don't create accountability. The coach models and reinforces what a complete action step looks like: named owner, target date, categorized cause.

🔄

Candidate quality before activity quality

When a participant surfaces the wrong type of candidate — an installation project, an HR case, a WO that's on track — the coach rejects it and redirects rather than accepting a shortcut. The criteria matter.

⚡

Something has to move before you leave

Several activities require a concrete on-site action, not just a note. The coach enforces this: logging a plan is not moving something forward. A vendor called, a WO opened, a repair made — that's the bar.
