03 / 05 · Coach Focus Areas

What the coach spends most of its time on

30 completed activities. Two recurring themes.

73%
Sessions with an explicit "not just X" redirect
53%
Sessions separating assumption from confirmed fact
40%
Sessions pushing for a specific date
Theme 1 — Naming a Real Owner and Date
Barriers get pinned to a person + date, not left at "the team is on it"
  • Participant lands on a group or vague timeframe first
  • Coach pushes one level further: who, specifically, by when
  • Pattern holds across sessions before the activity closes
"That's still 'the team,' not a named person."
Coach, to Manager, Production (1)
Theme 2 — Checking the Story First
Assumptions about a coworker get checked against evidence before acting
  • Participant arrives with a theory about why an issue recurs
  • Coach requires concrete instances first
  • Open question to the person, not an accusation
"Pull the actual evidence — that shows this is a real pattern, not just a feeling."
Coach, to Sr. Manager, Marketing

04 / 05 · Participant Dynamics

How participants engage — and where friction shows up

Right instincts, predictable friction points translating intent into action.

What's Working
Evidence-first structure → real cause often differs from the original assumption
"We started off on asking for help when needed. Then I was able to team her up with two of our experienced team members."
Director, Supply Chain
Where Friction Shows Up
Most common snag: group/system named as owner before a person is
  • "The team" or "the process" named first
  • Coach pushes a 2nd, 3rd time for a name
"The director said he will push his team to have it released before they leave."
Manager, Production (1)
Patterns by Role
  • Managers with an existing tracking habit move fastest (e.g. one participant's daily-reviewed report)
  • Directors: more often a first-time conversation, no existing system to point to
  • n=6 sample. Directional only
A Pattern Worth Watching
  • Self-reported outcomes don't always match the attached transcript
  • Example: one session’s outcome answer referenced an issue outside the coached conversation
  • Worth a gut-check on outcome-to-session mapping

05 / 05 · What the Coach Is Teaching Us

What this tells us about your participants

Not just activity completion — how this team thinks about accountability problems.

What the Data Suggests
Default lens: assume intent, not context
  • First instinct often blames attitude/character — "he thinks it's a waste of time," "not proactive"
  • Coach redirect surfaces a different cause: workload, unclear ownership, confidence
  • Team default: assume intent before checking context
  • Exactly the muscle this initiative targets
Peer Voice
Participants are already naming this shift themselves
  • "How quickly Doug was willing to act to find a solution with sales" — Sr. Manager, Marketing
  • "He provided me with his frustration in not getting answers or direction in the past." — Director, Engineering
  • "Yes, and I think both topics, overwhelmed and needs training are things that we need to focus on." — Director, Supply Chain
  • "It's usually how we work. And it's also how the notes are listed in the report." — Manager, Planning & Inventory Control
The Tension the Coach Is Navigating
Acting on a theory vs. checking it first
  • Nearly every session starts with a theory already in place
  • Coach's job: slow down, require evidence + a real conversation
  • Checking takes more time and more vulnerability than acting on gut
"Quick check: do you already have something concrete, or is it more of a gut sense right now?"
Coach, to Manager, Production (2)
