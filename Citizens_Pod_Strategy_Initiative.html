Overview
Initiative Structure
Three practices targeting the behavioral shift from siloed, individual execution to integrated pod-based collaboration across REO projects.

‚ö†Ô∏è The Business Challenge

REO projects are critically important ‚Äî often complex, high-stakes, and significant financial investments. Historically, timeline delays and project cost overruns have been recurring problems. Communication breaks down and inefficiencies emerge as a result of siloed work environments and postures. Team members default to legacy habits: working independently, handing off rather than collaborating, and raising issues late rather than early. The new pod strategy is designed to change all of that ‚Äî but moving from "knowing the new structure" to "actually working differently every day" requires deliberate practice, not just a rollout announcement.

Practice 1

Owning Your Role Within the Pod

Executing on your specific role expectations consistently ‚Äî delivering your outputs on time, proactively updating pod members on your progress, and flagging blockers early rather than waiting until deadlines slip. Moving from "my tasks" to "my contribution to the pod."

Practice 2

Collaborating Across Roles

Working with pod teammates across disciplines ‚Äî pulling in the right people at the right time, sharing information before being asked, and making decisions together instead of passing work over the wall. Breaking down the silos that carried over from the legacy model.

Practice 3

Driving Continuous Improvement

Identifying what's working and what's not within the pod, sharing effective techniques and best practices across pods, and providing honest feedback to strengthen the pod system ‚Äî turning everyday project execution into learning opportunities for the team.
