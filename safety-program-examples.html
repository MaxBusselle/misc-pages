Freeport‑McMoRan Mining
North America & Indonesia

Management Capabilities: Safety

Safety behaviors are defined on paper but not executed in the field. Superintendents, Managers, and H&S Professionals each have defined roles — but follow-up routines aren't running, field presence is inconsistent, and dashboard data isn't driving action at the department level.

Challenge
  • Safety behaviors defined on paper, not in the field
  • Superintendents lack consistent follow-up routines
  • Dashboard data not driving department-level action
Practices
  • H&S expectations & follow-up routines
  • Purposeful field presence & verification
  • Accountability from dashboard results
Outcomes
  • Safety expectations become lived routines
  • Field conditions verified and acted on
  • H&S system functions as designed
gategroup Aviation Catering
Global

Ramp Safety

Documented ramp safety violations aren't being systematically addressed. Repeat offenders continue at-risk behaviors because supervisory follow-through is inconsistent — coaching doesn't escalate to discipline, and enforcement isn't visible enough to signal that expectations are real.

Challenge
  • Documented violations go unaddressed
  • Repeat offenders continue at-risk behaviors
  • Enforcement not visible enough to signal consequences
Practices
  • Following through on repeat offenders
  • Making targeted ramp observations
  • Coordinating Supervisors & Safety Specialists
Outcomes
  • Repeat violations (same driver, 30 days) decline
  • Enforcement becomes consistent and visible
  • Supervisors and Safety Specialists operate as one system
PrimeFlight Aviation Ground Services
United States

Wheelchair Safety

Wheelchair attendants generate PrimeFlight's highest-volume safety incidents — slips, tip-overs, roll-aways. The gap isn't knowledge. Execution breaks down under time pressure and unpredictable passenger contact at a targeted set of focus airports.

Challenge
  • Highest-volume incidents: slips, tip-overs, roll-aways
  • Gap is execution under pressure, not knowledge
  • Incidents strain airline partner relationships
Practices
  • Equipment prep before passenger contact
  • Controlled navigation on jetbridges & thresholds
  • Situational awareness (P3, shaped after P1/P2)
Outcomes
  • Right behaviors executed at the right moments
  • Execution problems surface before incidents occur
  • Incident frequency at focus airports trends down
Bristol Myers Squibb Cell Therapy
Manufacturing

Equipment Cleaning Behaviors

Manufacturing associates know the SOPs but skip steps under production pressure, driving deviations and CAPAs. Every lot is a specific patient's therapy — the stakes are high and the margin for error is zero.

Challenge
  • Cleaning steps skipped under production pressure
  • Knowledge isn't the gap — execution is
  • Deviations & CAPAs compound audit and regulatory risk
Practices
  • Completing the cleaning sequence without shortcuts
  • Material handling at grade transitions
  • Airlock transfer discipline
Outcomes
  • Full cleaning sequence executed under pressure
  • Cleaning-related deviations & CAPAs trend down
  • Execution issues surface as feedback, not failed lots
PT Freeport Indonesia Underground Mining
Papua, Indonesia

Underground Vehicle Safety

Inconsistent safety practices during underground vehicle operations create serious personnel hazards. A persistent gap exists between required procedures and actual operator behavior in the field during mucking, blasting, and lifting operations.

Challenge
  • Inconsistent practice in underground vehicle operations
  • Personnel hazards in mucking, blasting & lifting
  • Gap between required procedures & operator behavior
Practices
  • Verifying equipment readiness & operator knowledge
  • Inspecting critical controls & observing behaviors
  • Stopping unsafe operations & investigating root causes
Outcomes
  • Critical vehicle safety systems verified before operation
  • Unsafe conditions identified and corrected in the field
  • Safety leadership shifts from reactive to proactive
