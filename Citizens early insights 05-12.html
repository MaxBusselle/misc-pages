What FMs are learning · 01 of 03

The customer lens
shifts what they see

These facility managers visit branches constantly — many managing 40 to 115 locations. They are experienced walkers. But walking as a customer activates a different mode of seeing than walking as the person responsible for the work orders.

The finds in this first wave tell that story clearly. Worn door handle finishes. A faded night drop sign. Old unused security equipment still hanging from a ceiling vestibule. Vestibule scuffs that have been there for months. These are not things that escaped inspection — they were visible on every visit. The program is changing the filter, not the frequency.

"Aesthetics matter — not just utility. I would suggest looking at everything top to bottom."
Sean Conroy, Senior Facility Manager

What FMs are learning · 02 of 03

360 is the habit,
not just the finding

Several FMs in this first wave had sharp instincts and real findings — but imperfect follow-through on where those findings landed. One logged a flooring gap via punch list to the GC. One texted a photo to their FM. One walked a pristine new fit-out and kept the photos on their phone.

In each case the coaching was consistent: 360 is the record that travels with the location. A punch list doesn't. A text message doesn't. Getting findings into the system — not just identifying them — is the behavior this program is building, and it's one of the harder shifts because the workarounds feel just as effective in the moment.

"Even when something's going to the GC or vendor directly, log it in 360 first. That's the record that travels with the location, not the punch list."
AI Coach — Joseph Dudar, Queensbury

What FMs are learning · 03 of 03

Landlord responsibility
≠ not my problem

Three FMs in this wave identified real issues — a broken thermostat, a separating cove base, a non-functioning parking structure — and moved to hand them off to the landlord without logging them first. The instinct makes sense: if it's their property, it's their record to keep.

The program is explicitly redirecting this. Log it in 360 first, then escalate. The 360 entry documents that the issue exists, records that you've initiated contact, and creates accountability on the landlord's end. Without it, the finding exists only in a phone call or an email — and it disappears when those do.

"Even on landlord items, make sure the 360 entry documents that it's LL responsibility and that you've initiated contact. That paper trail protects you."
AI Coach — Sean Conroy, 1215 Superior
