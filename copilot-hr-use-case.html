What the rating gap tells us
Manager > Learner gap
Managers consistently rated improvement higher than learners self-reported — particularly in workflow and communication domains. Managers see the behavior change before learners fully recognize it in themselves.
Data domain gap
Analyzing HR Data had the widest learner self-rating (2.4) — reflecting genuine friction with prompt precision for complex datasets. Learners knew it was hard. Managers still saw results.
Accessibility range
Average difficulty across all activities was 3.5 out of 10, indicating activities landed as real work — not training overhead — while remaining accessible to professionals across varying technical comfort levels.
Endorsement signal
A 7.1/10 recommendation rating across 200 completions is a strong peer endorsement signal — learners were actively recommending the approach to colleagues without being prompted to do so.

What This Story Tells Us

Three signals worth naming

Across 247 completions, three consistent patterns emerged that point toward durable AI adoption — not just activity completion.

Signal #1
Learners applied Copilot to live, urgent work — not invented tasks Participants consistently described using activities to solve problems already on their desks: RIF communications, employee engagement survey analysis, meeting summaries for real stakeholders. The program met them where they were, not where a curriculum assumed they'd be.
Signal #2
Managers saw the behavior change before learners claimed it The persistent gap between manager improvement ratings (higher) and learner self-ratings (lower) is a healthy signal — it suggests behavior is visibly changing at the work level, even when learners are still calibrating their own confidence. This is the leading indicator that matters.
Signal #3
Friction in the data domain is a content gap, not a motivation gap Learners rated Analyzing HR Data the hardest and lowest on satisfaction — but still completed 77 activities there, the most of any domain. The demand is real. What's needed is more scaffolding around prompt iteration for complex, real-world datasets with messy structure and multiple sources.
💡
What this means for Phase 2
The data is pointing toward two distinct Phase 2 priorities: (1) deepen the data analysis track with prompt iteration scaffolding specific to HR data structures like Dayforce exports and compensation benchmarks, and (2) harvest the communication wins already in progress — this is where manager-observed behavior change is highest and learner momentum is strongest. The groundwork for both is already in the field.
