02 — Strategic Context

Why safety leadership is a strategic priority at TEREX.

Manufacturing has one of the highest injury rates of any U.S. industry sector, and PPE is one of the few interventions where day-to-day manager behavior — not policy — determines outcomes. TEREX's focus on front-line managers reflects a deliberate decision to invest where the leverage is highest.

01

The injury data lives at the floor level.

Recordable incidents in manufacturing concentrate around hand, eye, and hearing exposure — exactly the categories PPE is designed to address. Safety policy alone has well-documented limits in changing daily behavior; the proximate variable is how supervisors run their shifts.

02

Front-line managers are the lever.

A front-line manager interacts with PPE-relevant decisions hundreds of times per shift — what they model, expect, and notice becomes the operating standard. Investing in manager capability is the most direct path to improving the conditions that produce or prevent injuries.

03

Culture is the durable outcome.

Programs that target individual workers tend to fade with attention. Programs that build manager capability change how the floor operates regardless of who's on it. TEREX's investment is structured to produce durable behavior change, not a campaign-cycle improvement.

03 — Program Architecture

How the initiative is constructed.

The program develops front-line managers as active safety leaders by training three repeatable practices, expressed across three PPE categories, and instrumented through three diagnostic lenses applied to field data. Each layer answers a different question: what managers do, where they do it, and what we learn from doing it.

A. The Three Practices What managers do

The behavioral backbone of the program. Each practice is a discrete, repeatable action managers train against — sequenced as a complete leadership cycle from setting the standard to sustaining it in real time.

Practice 01 — Model

Modeling Proper PPE Habits

The manager as the visible standard. PPE compliance starts with what supervisors wear and how they wear it — not as a rule, but as the unmistakable signal of what's expected on the floor.

Doing it well looks like
PPE on before entering the production area, every time, without comment.
Practice 02 — Set

Setting and Communicating Clear PPE Expectations

Making the standard explicit and shared. Managers translate site-wide policy into the specific expectations of their team, in language workers actually use, with reasoning workers can repeat.

Doing it well looks like
Pre-shift huddles where PPE is named in context — not recited from a poster.
Practice 03 — Monitor

Monitoring PPE During Your Shifts

Sustaining the standard in real time. Active observation across the shift — including high-risk transitions like breaks and shift changes — and consistent, low-friction correction when something slips.

Doing it well looks like
Walking the floor with intent, catching small drift before it becomes a habit.
B. The Three PPE Categories Where they do it

The three contexts in which each Practice plays out. Selected because they account for the highest concentration of recordable PPE-related exposure in the TEREX production environment.

Eyewear Welding, grinding, and cutting operations across all fabrication areas.
Gloves Hand-intensive assembly and material handling — where selection and condition matter as much as use.
Hearing Sustained-noise environments across powered-tool, pneumatic, and assembly-line work.
C. The Three-Gap Diagnostic What we learn from the data

Each manager activity generates field-level data. The three-gap framework is the lens used to interpret that data — separating issues of habit from issues of knowledge from issues of system.

Habit Gap
Workers know the rule and have the equipment, but routine behavior has drifted. The intervention is repetition and reinforcement.
Knowledge Gap
Workers do not understand the standard, the reasoning, or the specific equipment. The intervention is teaching.
System Gap
Equipment, supply, layout, or process makes compliance hard or impossible. The intervention is fixing the system.
