Three Key Learnings → Carried Into Wave 2
1
Break transitions are the highest-risk moment. Compliance drops most reliably when workers return from breaks — and recovers with direct supervisor intervention.
2
Manager corrections hold. Over 63% of documented corrections were confirmed still in place hours later — workers are receptive when supervisors engage directly.
3
Equipment condition was invisible. Worn gloves, cracked face shields, degraded respirators — none of it surfaced until the program created a structured check routine.

Looking Ahead
From a PPE Initiative to a
Broader Safety Program
The same behavior-change model that's working for PPE can extend across REV Group's safety agenda
The PPE initiative has demonstrated that on-the-job coaching, structured activity practice, and AI-personalized reinforcement can measurably shift supervisor behavior. These five areas represent natural next candidates — each has the same profile as PPE: high-stakes, habit-driven, and dependent on supervisor behavior to improve. These are possibilities, not commitments.
🏗️
Material Handling
Ergonomics, lifting technique, crane zone behavior, and load-handling habits across production lines. Repetitive motion and improper technique are leading injury drivers in vehicle manufacturing.
High Volume
🔒
Lockout / Tagout
Energy isolation compliance during maintenance and repair. One of the most critical and consistently cited gaps in manufacturing — and one where habit and culture matter as much as procedure.
High Risk
⚗️
Hazardous Materials & Chemical Handling
Solvents, adhesives, and chemical exposure habits. Already surfacing in PPE Wave 1 field data — Loctite 243 degrading gloves faster than procurement schedules account for is one example.
Active Risk
🆕
New Employee Onboarding Safety
A dedicated safety behavior track for the first 90 days — when risk is highest, habits are still forming, and the cost of a bad pattern being set early compounds over time.
Prevention
🚛
Vehicle Operation Safety
Forklift operation, power equipment, spotting protocols, and pedestrian safety in active production areas. Already showing up in Wave 2 activity data — spotters, wheel chocks, and backing procedures.
High Frequency
