Inside Session 1:
Establish expectations for your team
The first homework is to know what your team is responsible for before you can hold them accountable. Here’s what that looks like in FLiNT — for a Slot Shift Supervisor at Boyd.
Activity Deep Dive
How Establish your team’s expectations works
Clarify Expectations · Session 1
Slot Shift Supervisor → Slot Attendants
The supervisor turns “go review the job descriptions” from a self-directed homework item into a documented, AI-coached conversation — grounded in the Casino Operations competency matrix and tied to a real moment on their floor.
Basic
Know what each role is responsible for
Pull each slot attendant’s job description from the Leadership Toolkit. Before your next pre-shift huddle, be able to articulate the Guest Service and Attention to Detail expectations for each role without the document in hand.
Can state expectations unprompted
Intermediate
Have the expectations conversation
Have a 1-on-1 with one slot attendant. Walk through their job description together. Ask them to tell you what they believe their top expectations are — then confirm, correct, or build on it. Document it in FLiNT.
1 documented expectations conversation
Advanced
Create your team’s guidelines and purpose
Facilitate a team session where you co-create communication norms and a department purpose statement — cascaded from Boyd’s mission. Post them in the Leadership Toolkit for your shift.
Team guidelines + purpose statement posted
Leading Indicator
Supervisors who can articulate their team’s expectations unprompted — and have had the documented conversation — before their first performance discussion at Month 5.
Basic technique: know what each role is responsible for before you can hold anyone accountable to it.
Behavioral Execution
What the supervisor did
Successful
Opens the Leadership Toolkit, reviews each job profile before the next shift, and completes the FLiNT activity — noting the expectations they hadn’t been articulating clearly. Documents a specific team member example as evidence.
Partial
Reviews the job descriptions but defaults to general impressions rather than specific competency language. Marks the activity complete without a concrete example — system flags for follow-up before moving to Intermediate.
Struggle
Doesn’t know where to find the job profiles. Unsure what “Guest Service at the supervisor level” looks like differently than at the attendant level. FLiNT scaffolds deeper — this person isn’t penalized, they’re supported.
Same activity — but what changed for the team?
External Outcome
What happened on the floor
Good
Team members know exactly what their supervisor expects — not what they inferred from tone or rumor. The expectations conversation becomes the foundation for the first real performance discussion at Session 5.
Mixed
Supervisor has the toolkit knowledge but hasn’t had the explicit conversation yet. Team members are still guessing at priorities — especially around Attention to Detail and documentation accuracy on machine resets.
Poor
No shared understanding of expectations. When a guest service complaint surfaces or a machine maintenance error is logged, the supervisor has no documented baseline to point to — and no data to defend the accountability conversation.
Basic → Intermediate → Advanced
Each milestone develops a distinct capability. Supervisors prove one before advancing — and their manager signs off before the next session begins. This is the documentation Boyd University is currently missing.
