Short, personalized activities
in the flow of work.
Step 1

Step 2
Do the work — huddle, coach, walk
Step 3

Weekly
Loop

One activity. One real moment. Every week.

The activity attaches to work the supervisor was already going to do — the huddle, the coaching conversation, the floor walk.

Nothing extra on top of the day. The system learns from what each supervisor logs, and the next activity meets them where they actually are.

What this produces
Three outcomes at once.
Outcome 1
Standard work adherence
Supervisors don't just know the standard — they execute it, reflect on it, and get reinforcement on it every working week.
Habits form in weeks, not years.
Outcome 2
Proper habits & expectations
New supervisors and transferred-in veterans converge on the same ritual — instead of importing whatever pattern they ran somewhere else.
One Gallatin standard, lived daily.

Why Gallatin specifically
Three reasons this fits Gallatin
right now.
Reason 01 · Timing
The rituals are still forming.
There's nothing to retrain, only to shape. This is the cheapest, fastest moment in the plant's whole life to build the supervisor habit you want.
Reason 02 · Scale
~70 supervisors. The right size.
Large enough to produce real variance and meaningful signal. Small enough to coordinate as a single pilot under one leadership team.
Reason 03 · Stakes
The cost of getting it right is visible.
Ramp-up performance is already on the corporate radar. Anything that accelerates supervisor capability accelerates the moderation curve.
