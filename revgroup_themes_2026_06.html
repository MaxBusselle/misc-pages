Emerging Result

A Culture of Precision

The activities and coaching are beginning to move supervisors away from general PPE check-ins toward a more deliberate standard — naming the person, the equipment, and the task. Early evidence suggests this orientation is starting to take shape as a shared expectation across the cohort.

Emerging Result

Recognition Over Correction

The activities and coaching are beginning to surface a reorientation in how supervisors approach PPE leadership — catching people doing it right, not just catching violations. It is early, but the pattern is consistent across all three practices and multiple business units.

68
activities where supervisors actively recognized correct PPE behavior
8
activities that were correction-only — no recognition of what was working
29
supervisors who independently named recognition as the key insight to share
"Zero tolerance for face shield violations. Call out violators privately and commend adherence openly."
Supervisor Production · Capacity

Emerging Result

A Culture of Vigilance

Through repeated activities and coaching, supervisors are beginning to name complacency — not knowledge gaps or missing equipment — as the real obstacle to sustained PPE safety. That shift in how they frame the problem is itself an early sign of growing ownership of the safety standard.

21
supervisors who independently named complacency or consistency as the real challenge
37
supervisors actively building the repetition that counters drift
3
business units represented — Fleetwood, Goldshield, Capacity and more
"My team will never get a free pass on grinding safety. I received twenty-four stitches to my face because of a grinding accident without a face shield."
Production Lead · Goldshield
What supervisors are sharing about vigilance
"Everyone gets complacent. Continued review for PPE is needed."

"Remind folks that the time in which an injury occurs will be when the team member gets complacent — or left gloves somewhere and didn't want to walk the whole way back."
