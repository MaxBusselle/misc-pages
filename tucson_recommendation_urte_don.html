How the plug-in hybrid works

Four Drive Modes

Most daily driving around Pasadena — errands, appointments, short trips — will run on electricity alone. Plug in at home each night and wake up with a full charge. No special infrastructure needed.

⚡
EV Mode

Pure electric only. Silent, zero emissions, no gas used whatsoever. Battery power only.

Best for local errands
🔄
Auto Mode

The car decides. Prioritizes electric and switches to gas only when needed. Recommended for everyday use.

Best for everyday driving
⛽
HEV Mode

Gas and electric work together. Deliberately saves battery charge for specific situations later in the trip.

Best for highway trips
🏁
Sport Mode

Maximum power from both gas and electric simultaneously. Sharpens throttle response when you need it.

SmartLifter LC Wheelchair Hoist

The SmartLifter LC installs directly inside the Tucson's cargo area and lifts the wheelchair in at the touch of a button. No manual lifting, no dismantling, no effort required.

The Tucson's 38.7 cu ft cargo area — nearly 50% larger than the RDX — gives the SmartLifter ample room to operate. The low, flat load floor and wide cargo opening are ideal for the hoist arm's swing-out motion.

  1. 1
    Open the hands-free liftgate — wave foot under bumper, gate opens on its own
  2. 2
    SmartLifter arm swings out from the cargo area automatically
  3. 3
    Attach the wheelchair to the hoist hook
  4. 4
    Press the button — wheelchair lifts, rotates, and stows in ~60 seconds
  5. 5
    Close the liftgate — restraint system holds the wheelchair securely during travel
💡 When the hoist isn't needed, the lifting arm detaches from the frame in seconds, reclaiming the full cargo area. Installation requires a certified mobility equipment dealer using a Tucson-specific fitting kit. MobilityWorks (LA area) handles SmartLifter LC sales and installation.
