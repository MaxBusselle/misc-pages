Example Activity · Food Safety
Observe a Receiving Temperature Check
🎯 During the next food delivery receiving period, observe one staff member conducting a receiving temperature check — noting their technique, documentation timing, and accuracy.
Instructions
Receiving temperature checks are where food safety starts — if items aren't verified at the dock, everything downstream is built on assumptions. Schedule a 10-minute observation during the next food delivery receiving period.
1. Bring the PRP-3 documentation form and verify the staff member has correct documentation and a calibrated thermometer.
2. Observe them performing temperature checks on 3–4 different food items. Note technique and accuracy:
✅ Inserting probe into thickest part, waiting for reading to stabilize, recording immediately
❌ Quick surface touch, recording before stabilization, batching entries at end of shift
3. Confirm they record temperatures on the PRP-3 form immediately after each measurement — not after completing all checks or at the end of the shift.
4. Provide immediate feedback — acknowledge what was done well and correct any issues observed.
Survey questions would ask: Did you observe the full check? Did the staff member record immediately? What technique issues did you notice? What feedback did you provide?

Example Activity · Cleaning & Sanitization
Check for Missed Surfaces Before Starting Contact Time
🎯 After applying sanitizer to galley equipment or catering cart surfaces, inspect from multiple angles and re-spray any missed areas before starting the required contact time.
Instructions
Surfaces are still getting missed — undersides of trolley shelves, edges of galley counters, recessed areas around latches. Add a deliberate inspection between spraying and starting the contact time clock.
For your next 2–3 equipment cleaning tasks, focus on the moment right after you spray:
1. Apply sanitizer to all surfaces as you normally would — working around the item at the correct distance.
2. Before starting the contact time, stop and inspect. Tilt, rotate, or open the item to see every face — especially the underside of shelves, behind handles, and around hinges. Look for the contrast between wet (covered) and dry (missed).
✅ Tilting a trolley shelf under the light and spotting a dry patch on the bottom edge — then re-spraying before noting start time
❌ Spraying, glancing from one angle, and starting the clock — a quick look won't catch what's underneath
3. Re-spray any dry areas. Then note the start time — the contact time begins after you've confirmed full coverage.
4. Complete the rest of the sequence — full contact time, wipe-down, air dry.
⭐ The inspection adds seconds, not minutes — but it prevents running a full contact time on a partially covered surface.
Survey questions would ask: Did you inspect before starting contact time? How many surfaces needed re-spraying? What areas are most commonly missed? Describe what you found on inspection.
