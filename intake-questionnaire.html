Question 1 of 9

What's the business challenge
you're trying to solve?

If things don't change, what happens? Be as specific as you can — real numbers, real consequences.

Question 2 of 9

Who needs to change most?

Who in your organization has the biggest impact on this problem — and what do they do day to day? Roughly how many of them are there?

Question 3 of 9

What are the performance gaps?

What are people doing wrong — or not doing at all — that's making this a problem? Specific examples are especially helpful here.

Question 4 of 9

What do your top performers
do differently?

Think about the best people in this role. What separates them from everyone else — specific habits, routines, or behaviors?

Question 5 of 9

What would managers start noticing
if this actually worked?

If behavior genuinely changed a few months in — what would managers observe? What looks different on the floor, in meetings, in the numbers?

Question 6 of 9

What does big-picture success
look like in a year?

Zoom out twelve months. If the whole team improves at this, what changes about your results — business metrics, culture, outcomes?

Question 7 of 9

What do you already track
or measure?

What shows up on dashboards, in reviews, or in regular reports? What data do you already pay attention to in this area?

Question 8 of 9

Walk us through a typical week
in this role.

The meetings, calls, tasks, and interactions that come around regularly — the more detail here, the better we can design around their actual rhythm.

Question 9 of 9

Are there other roles that might
benefit from this too?

Managers, peers, adjacent teams — anyone else in the organization who faces a similar challenge or who would benefit from this kind of development?

Final thoughts

Anything else we should know?

Context we haven't asked about, sensitivities to be aware of, constraints, history — anything that would help us come into the conversation prepared.
