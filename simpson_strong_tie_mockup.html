Practice Deep Dive
How Leading Accountable Daily Huddles works
Practice 1
Leading Accountable Daily Huddles
The supervisor turns the 7:30 huddle from a one-way broadcast into the team's accountability ritual — issues get owned, operators get prompted by name, and repeat problems get diagnosed instead of re-assigned.
Basic
Close the loop
Walk every prior-day action item. Attach a named owner and a date to every new issue before the huddle ends.
Huddles ending with every item owned & dated
Intermediate
Ask a named operator
Direct one open-ended question to a specific operator about today's risks — and hold silence until they answer. No more "anybody have anything?"
Named prompts producing substantive input
Advanced
Frame before fixing
When an issue reappears — air in the lines, videojet stopping — pause and walk the team through what changed and what's common across occurrences before assigning the next action.
Repeat issues diagnosed, not re-assigned

What an Activity Looks Like
A real activity from Leading Accountable Daily Huddles
Basic Core · Difficulty 2
Close the Loop at Your Next Huddle
🎯 Before and during your next 7:30 huddle, close out every prior-day action item and attach a named owner and a date to each new issue raised.
1. Before the huddle — pull up yesterday's tracker and mark anything you already know is done. ✅ "Shift 2 confirmed the pallet count before close — marking closed"
2. For items still open, note which operator or lead you expect to update on each — so you know who you're looking at when you bring it up.
3. During the huddle — walk each open prior-day item. ✅ "Temperature readings on Vessel 3 — Jose, you said you'd pull the cal sheet. Closed, or carrying to Thursday?"
4. As new issues come up, attach a name and a date before moving on. Don't let the next topic start until both land on the tracker. ✅ "Videojet stopped on your line — Martinez, by Wednesday end-of-shift"
5. ⭐ If an issue has no frontline owner — it's a document or engineering problem (like the 120°F vs. 90°F mix discrepancy) — assign it to yourself to escalate. Don't leave it unowned.

Upper-Remedial · Difficulty 3
Put a Date on Every Item
🎯 Before and during your next 7:30 huddle, make sure every action item — prior-day or new — has a specific date next to the name, not just an owner.
1. Before the huddle — scan yesterday's tracker and circle every item that has an owner but no date. Those are your repair items. ✅ "Martinez — vat log update. Circled. No date."
2. Decide a default cadence you'll fall back to when you don't know the right date — "end of today's shift" or "by tomorrow's huddle" covers most cases.
3. During the huddle — walk the no-date items first and lock a date on each one with the owner in the room. ✅ "Martinez, you've got the vat log — end of today's shift, or tomorrow morning?"
4. As new issues come up, ask for the date immediately after assigning the owner. Don't move on without both. ✅ "Chen on the Line 2 videojet, by Wednesday close. On the board."
Why? If your last huddle ended with owners named but dates soft, this activity targets the exact gap — dating in the moment, with the owner in the room. Ownership without a date is half-tracked.
