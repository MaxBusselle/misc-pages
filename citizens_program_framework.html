Customer focus

The branch experience drives customer trust and retention. When facilities teams don't see the branch through the customer's eyes, work gets prioritized by habit instead of by what customers actually notice.

Fresh EyesLive

FMS TechLS Lead

  • noticing what a customer sees first
  • turning observations into fixes

Building Your Team's Quality Eye

DirFM

Leaders don't develop the quality eye in their people; the standard for what good looks like isn't taught down the line.

Practices
  • modeling the standard
  • spot-checking with the team
  • giving quality feedback

Prioritizing Work

FMFTLS

Work is done in the order it arrives, not by customer impact.

Practices
  • triage by customer impact
  • prioritize the backlog by what customers notice
  • sequence around branch-open hours

Peak-moment readiness

FMLS Lead

Practices

The customer's seat

Holding vendors accountable

FMFM Leads

Program 05 · 4 initiatives

Managing projects

Projects in Real Estate Operations (branch refreshes, remodels, relocations, equipment replacements) are often run late, over budget, or disruptive, which raises cost, disrupts branch operations, and hurts the customer.

Keeping everyone in the loop

FMPODDir

Branch managers and regional leaders aren't kept informed, so project disruption comes as a surprise.

Practices
  • map the stakeholders
  • set an update cadence
  • communicate scope and timeline up front
  • escalate risks early

Managing a portfolio of projects

PODFM Leads

With many concurrent projects, attention is spread thin and things get dropped.

Practices
  • triage active projects
  • run a weekly status review
  • track timeline and budget across projects
  • reallocate attention to what's at risk

Seeing problems before they blow up

PODS Tech

Risks and issues aren't anticipated or caught early, so small problems become expensive ones.

Practices
  • build a risk list at kickoff
  • surface constraints and dependencies early
  • run a weekly issue check
  • keep mitigation plans ready

Learning across projects

PODS Tech

The same mistakes repeat project to project because nothing is captured.

Practices
  • run a real closeout (punch list, warranties, docs)
  • capture lessons
  • feed them into the next scope
  • build a reusable playbook
